About Us

The earliest form of perfume was incense, which was burned to release its scent. The word "perfume" comes from the Latin phrase per fumum, which means "through smoke". The first liquid perfumes were made as early as 3500 BC. Modern perfumery began in the late 19th century, when artificial compounds like vanillin and coumarin were commercially synthesized. These compounds allowed for a wider range of scents than natural materials.Perfumes are a combination of natural scents, synthetic chemicals, fixatives, and alcohol. They can be made from fragrant essential oils derived from plants and spices, or from synthetic aromatic compounds.Perfumes are used in many cosmetic and toiletry products, such as soaps, deodorants, face powders, and bath oils. They can also be used in paints and cleaning materials to mask unpleasant odors, or to give them a distinctive scent. For example, leather odors can be added to furniture coverings, and bread odors can be added to wrapping paper. Perfumes smell stronger when it's warmer, so people often apply them to their neck, wrist, and back of the knee, which are warmer areas of the body.Fragrances can help evoke pleasant feelings and improve overall well-being. They can also be associated with nostalgia, memory, and self-image.Perfume is a fragrance that creates a sense of ambiance and aroma around a person. It is a scented product that contains a mixture of essential oils, aromatic compounds, and solvents. The scent of perfume has long been a symbol of sophistication, elegance, and individuality. Perfume has been an indulgence for centuries, and its significance has not faded. The perfume industry has become a global venture, with various scents, brands, and fragrances to choose from.Perfumes come in various types, including floral, woody, oriental, spicy, and citrusy.
